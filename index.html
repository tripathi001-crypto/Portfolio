The Problem
Every mock interview tool felt like a chatbot, not a coach
Job seekers were spending months prepping using generic tools that gave surface-level feedback — "good answer!" — with zero PM-specific depth. No structure coaching. No framework gaps called out. No sense of what a real hiring panel would actually think.
"Tools were optimizing for engagement, not for the user actually getting hired."
↓
02
💡
The Insight
ATS optimization gets you the interview. Confidence gets you the offer.
Most tools pick one — resume OR interview. But the real drop-off happens in the gap between: a candidate gets shortlisted, then freezes in the room. I saw both problems were part of one journey, and nobody was solving the full loop.
User research: 40+ conversations Drop-off point: post-shortlist Gap: resume → room confidence
↓
03
⚙️
The Decision
Build one platform that handles both — with AI that thinks like a PM interviewer
I chose React + OpenAI APIs and deliberately scoped v1 to two jobs: (1) scan your resume against real JDs and flag ATS gaps, (2) run a structured mock interview that probes like a senior PM would — STAR, product sense, metrics. No fluff. No generic "great job." Real, specific, actionable feedback.

MakeMyTrip · B2C Travel

Enabling Seamless Group Travel

How do you get 6 friends to agree on a flight — and actually book it together?

User Research Journey Mapping PRD B2C UX Design
🔍
The Problem
Group travel is broken by design

Individual booking flows assume one decision-maker. In reality, group trips involve multiple people, different budgets, conflicting preferences, and endless WhatsApp threads. MakeMyTrip had no native way to handle this — users were bouncing to spreadsheets and screenshots to coordinate.

💡
The Insight
The coordinator is the real user — not the group

Research revealed one person always takes charge — the "trip organiser." The product needed to serve them first: give them tools to propose options, collect votes, and lock in decisions — without the group ever needing to leave the app.

Pain: 3+ platforms used per booking
Drop-off: decision paralysis in groups
Insight: one organiser, many reactors
⚙️
The Solution
A collaborative booking layer built on top of existing flows

Designed a group booking module with shareable trip proposals, in-app preference voting, budget range pooling, and a group lock-in mechanism — all without disrupting the existing solo booking experience.

📈
The Impact
Reduced coordination friction, increased group conversion
↓60%Decision time
1 appvs 3+ platforms
+35%Group booking intent

Shaadi.com · B2C Matchmaking

Building Trust Through Verification

In a space where trust is everything — how do you design a badge system that feels safe, not intrusive?

Trust & Safety UX Design Verification Systems B2C
🔍
The Problem
Fake profiles were destroying user trust — and retention

On a platform where users make one of life's biggest decisions, fake or misrepresented profiles weren't just a UX problem — they were a safety and business risk. Users, especially women, were disengaging after early matches felt fraudulent.

💡
The Insight
Trust is felt before it's read — it's visual, not textual

Users don't read verification disclaimers. They scan profiles for visual signals. The insight was to design a tiered badge system that communicated credibility at a glance, with progressive disclosure for users who wanted more detail.

Pain: disengagement after fake matches
Insight: trust is visual, not textual
Design: tiered badges + progressive disclosure
⚙️
The Solution
A 3-tier trust badge framework with friction-right verification

Designed a badge system with three levels — ID Verified, Photo Verified, Premium Verified — each with distinct visual treatment. Key decision: higher trust badges required more steps, but the UX made that feel like a privilege, not a burden.

📈
The Impact
Higher engagement with verified profiles, lower disengagement
+42%Engagement on verified profiles
↓28%Fake profile reports
3-tierTrust badge framework
